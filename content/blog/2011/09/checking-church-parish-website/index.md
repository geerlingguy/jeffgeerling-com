<h4>Lisa Hendey</h4>

This presentation was given at the <a href="http://cnmc.sqpn.com/">2011 Catholic New Media Conference</a>. Below are my notes on the presentation:

<h4>Why Bother?</h4>

<ul>
	<li>Educate about the Church</li>
	<li>Provide parish information</li>
	<li>Outreach to interest groups</li>
	<li>Build community</li>
</ul>

<h4>10 Best Practices for Parish Websites</h4>

From Craig Berry:

<ol>
	<li>Useful contact us page</li>
	<li>About Us/History Page</li>
	<li>Prominent use of Social Media</li>
	<li>Online Map/Directions</li>
	<li>Calendar of Events</li>
	<li>Weekly Bulletins<!--break--></li>
	<li>Blog</li>
	<li>Site Map</li>
	<li>Mass Times</li>
	<li>Donate Online</li>
</ol>

<h4>What Parishioners Want (from online polling)</h4>

<ol>
	<li>Priestly presence (this can be easy or hard)</li>
	<li>Ministry schedule information</li>
	<li>Homilies available online</li>
	<li>Q&amp;A with Pastor</li>
	<li>Photos, photos, photos!</li>
	<li>Religious education resources</li>
	<li>Opt-in text reminders</li>
	<li>Bulletin online early</li>
	<li>Mobile version</li>
	<li>Online forms and registration</li>
	<li>Active Facebook presence for wired parishioners and teens</li>
	<li>Online stewardship</li>
	<li>Ride sharing resources</li>
</ol>

<h4>Outreach</h4>

<ul>
	<li>We are at the service of the Word.</li>
	<li>Never neglect human relationships.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Is social media right for your parish?</h4>

From Jared Dees, <em>Today's Parish</em>

<ul>
	<li>What are my parish's goals?</li>
	<li>Does the parish website align with these goals?</li>
	<li>Do I have the time and people to participate in social media?</li>
	<li>Will I be able to relinquish control of the conversation?</li>
	<li>Are you ready?</li>
</ul>

<h4>Building Community</h4>

<ul>
	<li>Involve many and non-traditional volunteers</li>
	<li>Offer cultural and linguistic diversity</li>
	<li>Include photos (within existing usage policies)</li>
	<li>Meet them where they are</li>
	<li>Establish a parish hastag</li>
	<li>Activate youth and young adult resources and don't forget "wired" seniors</li>
	<li>Hang Out (on Google+)</li>
	<li>Find a Knight in shining armor</li>
	<li>Check in at church</li>
</ul>

<h4>Dealing with Resistance</h4>

<ul>
	<li>What are some of the best practices for overcoming resistance in social media efforts in the parish?

<ul>
	<li>Parish leaders are often unable to see the online component of the parish as more than a 'publishing medium'</li>
	<li>Assure leaders that you can put limits and boundaries on things so it doesn't grow out of control or too expensive</li>
	<li>You can still have active and beautiful online presences without vast resources</li>
	<li>Start small, and incrementally grow. Sometimes you just need to take a small step, and then wait.</li>
</ul></li>
</ul>
