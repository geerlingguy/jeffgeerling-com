<p>
	Some of the crazy thoughts that go through my head... yesterday I was watching some of the other teams play at De Smet (where I&#39;ll be coaching the freshmen volleyball team this year), and I thought of a relevant comparison between HTTP/1.1 status codes and volleyball plays.</p>
<ul>
	<li>
		<strong>101 Switching Protocols</strong> - A play&#39;s not working? Move a couple guys around.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>200 OK</strong> - Good job. We got the point.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>300 Multiple Choices</strong> - The setter always has a few...</li>
	<li>
		<strong>304 Not Modified</strong> - When a hitter misses the ball.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>305 Use Proxy</strong> - When the setter is out of the play.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>307 Temporary Redirect</strong> - A tip over the net.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>400 Bad Request</strong> - Setter/hitter confusion.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>401 Unauthorized</strong> - The libero tries hitting the ball above the plane of the net.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>403 Forbidden</strong> - A blocker stuffs his opponent.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>404 Not Found</strong> - When the ball hits the floor and nobody moves to pass it.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>405 Method Not Allowed</strong> - A lift.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>406 Not Acceptable</strong> - A missed serve.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>408 Request Timeout</strong> - Self-explanatory.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>409 Conflict</strong> - These require the referee&#39;s intervention.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>417 Expectation Failed</strong> - When the players don&#39;t live up to the coach&#39;s expectations.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>500 Server Error</strong> - See &quot;406 Not Acceptable&quot;</li>
	<li>
		<strong>502 Bad Gateway</strong> - A poor set.</li>
	<li>
		<strong>503 Service Unavailable</strong> - Anytime a middle hitter is serving.</li>
</ul>
<p>
	More status codes: <a href="http://www.w3.org/Protocols/rfc2616/rfc2616-sec10.html">HTTP/1.1 Status Code Definitions</a></p>
